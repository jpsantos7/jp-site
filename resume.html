My Professional Background

I have worked as a freelance designer while still studying. Most of my work those years was on graphic and editorial design, logo creation/concept. I was also able to do some work on the marketing side of things, creating numerous marketing materials and publicity for event campaigns. I also dabbled in some prototyping (low-res and hi-def mockups) for a number of projects.

While I was still writing my master’s degree I got the opportunity to do an internship in which I started learning most of my web design skills because now I could apply the design concepts I had learnt to the web and gain lots of experience on HTML5, CSS3, SEO, Site speed optimization, prototyping and whatnot for a number of different projects with different sizes.

Later on that same year I had the amazing opportunity to join the company where I did the internship in and I’ve been there ever since.

The projects in which I worked while on this company were very varied and with very different scales. They ranged from an art gallery website to the total UI/UX for a Rich Internet application to control trains and train stations that was implemented in various locations around the globe.

During these work years I learned a little bit of some things and a lot of others such as HTML5, CSS, Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Premiere, Adobe Indesign, Adobe Fireworks, Ruby, Ruby on Rails, Javascript, HAML, SASS, LESS, Bootstrap, Git, React.js, React Native and many others. I also had to work on different development environments (Windows, Linux, OSX and VMs).

I also had the opportunity to improve my English by doing Brazilian Portuguese translations and also a bit of Spanish translations.

I’m always trying to find new exciting ways of doing new things. I consider it paramount to keep up to date with new technologies, trends and methodologies in today’s very fast paced world in order to provide always better, cleaner and simpler solutions.
